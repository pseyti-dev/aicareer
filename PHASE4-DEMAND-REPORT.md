---

## 1. Is there real search volume?

**Yes — and it is growing.** The AI job displacement query space is one of the highest-interest informational categories of 2026:

- "Will AI replace [career]" queries are among the top-searched career informational intents
- AI skills demand in job postings has **tripled since 2023** (1 in 10 postings now require AI skills)
- Workers with AI skills earn **56% more** — driving enormous demand for career guidance content
- Multiple authoritative sites now compete directly: Washington Post interactive tool, TechTarget, BCG, TripleTen calculator, FindSkill.ai, Articuler.ai

**Assessment: Demand is validated. The space is large and growing.**

---

## 2. Are AI Overviews cannibalizing these queries?

**Partially — but we have a structural advantage.**

Key data (Google AI Overviews statistics, 2026):

- **48% of all searches** now show an AI Overview
- For **informational queries**: 36% trigger AI Overviews
- Career/education category: jumped from 18% to **83% AIO coverage** in 12 months (+361%)
- Traffic to information publishers: down 25–60% (HubSpot lost ~70–80% organic traffic)

**BUT:** Brands cited _inside_ AI Overviews earn **120% more organic clicks** vs. uncited competitors. The AEO work already done (llms.txt, FAQPage schema, Dataset schema, structured Q&A) directly positions us to be cited in AI Overviews rather than displaced by them.

**Assessment: High AIO risk for thin informational pages. Mitigated for original-data and tool-intent pages.**

## 5. What Phase 5 should build (if approved)

Phase 5 is NOT "generate 100 thin pages." It is a **precision expansion** of existing career pages, adding:

1. **Deeper "will AI replace [career]?" content** on existing `/risk/[slug]/` pages — each page already exists, just needs the informational content layer that makes it citable in AI Overviews

3. **An "AI skills by career" index** — bridges the risk pages to the upskilling affiliate funnel

Every new content block must pass the CLAUDE.md §5 quality gate.  
Zero thin pages. No find-replace templates.
